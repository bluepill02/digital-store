# Personalization Variables

Fill these once. Reuse them across all 5 prompts in this bundle.

## Required variables (12)

| # | Variable | What it is | Example |
|---|----------|------------|---------|
| 1 | `[NEWSLETTER_NAME]` | The name of your newsletter | "The Pipeline" |
| 2 | `[PRODUCT_NAME]` | The product or service behind the newsletter | "Loomsy" |
| 3 | `[PRODUCT_ONE_LINE]` | What your product does in plain English | "Async standup tool for distributed engineering teams" |
| 4 | `[ICP_ROLE]` | The specific job title of your reader | "Engineering manager at a 20-200 person tech company" |
| 5 | `[ICP_PAIN]` | The one pain your reader has that your product solves | "Spending 2 hours/day in standups that produce no decisions" |
| 6 | `[FOUNDER_NAME]` | Your name (or pen name) for the story email | "Sam Miller" |
| 7 | `[FOUNDER_BIO_ONE_LINE]` | One line of credibility | "Built two devtools previously acquired" |
| 8 | `[ORIGIN_STORY_HOOK]` | The 2-sentence version of why you started the company | "I watched three engineering managers I respect burn out trying to scale async. I built Loomsy so they wouldn't have to choose between shipping and sleeping." |
| 9 | `[PROOF_POINT_1]` | A specific, quantified result a customer got | "Cut standup time from 90 min/day to 12 min/day at Acme Corp (180 engineers)" |
| 10 | `[PROOF_POINT_2]` | A second proof point or named customer | "Used by teams at Vercel, Linear, and Supabase" |
| 11 | `[QUICK_WIN_ACTION]` | One small thing the reader can do today that proves your thesis | "Run a 15-min 'decision-only' meeting with your team this week and reply with what happened" |
| 12 | `[SOFT_CTA]` | Where you want them to go next (NOT a hard sell) | "Try Loomsy free for 14 days, or just keep reading - either way I'm glad you're here." |

## Optional variables (improves output if filled)

| # | Variable | What it is |
|---|----------|------------|
| 13 | `[INDUSTRY_VERTICAL]` | Your specific vertical (devtools, fintech, martech, etc.) |
| 14 | `[NEWSLETTER_CADENCE]` | How often your main newsletter goes out |
| 15 | `[COMPETITOR_NAMES]` | 1-3 alternative tools readers might be using |
| 16 | `[YOUR_TONE]` | 2-3 words describing your voice (e.g. "dry, technical, opinionated") |
| 17 | `[TABOO_PHRASES]` | Words you never use (e.g. "synergy", "leverage", "unlock") |

## How to fill them

- Block 30 minutes. Don't try to do it between meetings - the prompts work much better when the inputs are sharp.
- If you don't have a number for proof points, skip them rather than fabricate. The prompts have fallbacks ("if no specific number, use a credible qualitative claim").
- Be specific. "Engineering manager" is good. "Tech professional" is not.

## Sanity check

Before pasting into the prompts, read your 12 variables out loud as if to a friend. If it sounds like marketing copy, rewrite until it sounds like you're explaining your business to someone you respect at a dinner party.
