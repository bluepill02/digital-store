# Prompt 4 - Welcome Email (Origin Story)

**When to send:** Day 7.

**Purpose:** Humanize the brand. People subscribe to people, not newsletters. After a week of tactical content, the reader is ready to hear why this exists.

---

## The prompt

```
You are writing day 7 of a 5-email welcome sequence for a B2B SaaS
newsletter. The reader has received three tactical emails. They've
decided you're worth paying attention to. Now you earn the right to
tell them why you exist.

Voice: personal, specific, unpolished in the ways that signal honesty.
Avoid the founder-story cliches ("I saw a problem", "I knew there had
to be a better way"). Skip any sentence that could appear in another
company's About page.

Variables:
- FOUNDER_NAME = [FOUNDER_NAME]
- FOUNDER_BIO_ONE_LINE = [FOUNDER_BIO_ONE_LINE]
- ORIGIN_STORY_HOOK = [ORIGIN_STORY_HOOK]
- ICP_PAIN = [ICP_PAIN]
- PRODUCT_ONE_LINE = [PRODUCT_ONE_LINE]
- NEWSLETTER_NAME = [NEWSLETTER_NAME]

What this email MUST do:
1. Open with a specific scene - a moment, a conversation, a meeting, a
   Slack message. Time, place, one or two people. Avoid "Once upon a
   time" energy. Concrete beats abstract.
2. Tell the ORIGIN_STORY_HOOK in your own words but keep its core. This
   is the why-built-this beat. Maximum 3 sentences.
3. Explain what's been hard. One paragraph on what surprised you, what
   you got wrong, or what you didn't understand at first. Vulnerability
   builds trust faster than competence.
4. Connect the story to the reader. "If you're ICP_ROLE dealing with
   ICP_PAIN, I built [PRODUCT_NAME] because I think that pain is real
   and the existing options don't take it seriously enough."
5. End with one line that signals this newsletter is a personal project,
   not a marketing channel. ("I write this on Sundays. - [FOUNDER_NAME]")

What this email MUST NOT do:
- Open with "Hi, I'm [FOUNDER_NAME] and I..." - skip the formal intro,
  we already have their attention.
- Use more than one em-dash in the body.
- Be longer than 400 words. Stories should still feel like emails.
- Use the phrase "fast forward to today".
- Mention your funding, team size, press coverage, or any other status
  marker. The story should be about the work, not the wins.
- Include a CTA. This email earns the right to be cta-free.

Output format: plain text. Subject line, then email body. Under 400 words.
Reply with only the email.
```
