# Prompt 3 - Welcome Email (Quick Win)

**When to send:** Day 4.

**Purpose:** Get the reader to take one small action in the next 24 hours. Engagement now predicts retention for months. This is the highest-leverage email in the sequence.

---

## The prompt

```
You are writing day 4 of a 5-email welcome sequence for a B2B SaaS
newsletter. By now the reader has gotten the delivery and proof emails.
This email's job is to get them to DO something in the next 24 hours -
one small action that takes under 15 minutes and produces a visible
result.

Voice: tactical, specific, slightly opinionated. You're not lecturing,
you're sharing a tip with a colleague. Brevity matters more than polish.

Variables:
- NEWSLETTER_NAME = [NEWSLETTER_NAME]
- ICP_ROLE = [ICP_ROLE]
- ICP_PAIN = [ICP_PAIN]
- QUICK_WIN_ACTION = [QUICK_WIN_ACTION]
- PRODUCT_ONE_LINE = [PRODUCT_ONE_LINE]

What this email MUST do:
1. Open by acknowledging ICP_PAIN as the thing they're dealing with this
   week. Use their language back to them.
2. Describe ONE specific QUICK_WIN_ACTION - what to do, how long it takes,
   what the result looks like. Be concrete enough that they could start
   without asking you a clarifying question.
3. Briefly explain WHY this action works (the underlying principle, in
   2-3 sentences). Skip the principle if the action is self-evident.
4. Give a specific time window ("before Friday") so the action has a
   deadline.
5. Ask them to reply with what happened. Make the reply ask small and
   specific ("Reply with what your team did - or why you didn't do it.
   Either answer is useful to me.").

What this email MUST NOT do:
- Be longer than 300 words. Quick wins should feel quick.
- Include more than one action. One is a tip. Two is a task list.
- Link to a blog post. This email is the value. No outbound links.
- Soft-sell the product. If QUICK_WIN_ACTION happens to involve the
  product, fine. Otherwise, do not mention the product.

Output format: plain text. Subject line, then email body. Under 300 words.
Reply with only the email.
```
