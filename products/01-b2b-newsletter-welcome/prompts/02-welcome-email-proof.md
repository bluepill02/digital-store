# Prompt 2 - Welcome Email (Proof)

**When to send:** Day 2.

**Purpose:** Establish credibility before trust decays. One specific customer result beats ten testimonials.

---

## The prompt

```
You are writing day 2 of a 5-email welcome sequence for a B2B SaaS
newsletter. The reader got the delivery email yesterday. They're now
deciding whether you actually know what you're talking about.

Voice: matter-of-fact, confident, no chest-thumping. Avoid superlatives
("the best", "the leading", "industry-leading"). Show, don't tell.

Variables (substitute these):
- NEWSLETTER_NAME = [NEWSLETTER_NAME]
- PRODUCT_NAME = [PRODUCT_NAME]
- ICP_ROLE = [ICP_ROLE]
- ICP_PAIN = [ICP_PAIN]
- PROOF_POINT_1 = [PROOF_POINT_1]
- PROOF_POINT_2 = [PROOF_POINT_2]
- YOUR_TONE = [YOUR_TONE]
- TABOO_PHRASES = [TABOO_PHRASES] (comma-separated, or empty)

What this email MUST do:
1. Open with a specific, quantified customer outcome. Lead with PROOF_POINT_1
   in the first sentence. No throat-clearing.
2. Briefly explain the mechanism - what did the customer DO that produced
   this result. One short paragraph.
3. Include a second signal of credibility: PROOF_POINT_2 (named customers,
   a number, a timeframe, a category leader rank - pick whichever is real).
4. Pivot to the reader in the last paragraph: "If you're dealing with
   ICP_PAIN, here's what I think the underlying issue usually is..."
   Offer ONE specific reframe or observation they haven't heard before.
5. End with one question that invites a reply. Not a soft CTA - a real
   question. Questions about ICP_PAIN or their current setup work well.

What this email MUST NOT do:
- Use any word in TABOO_PHRASES.
- Open with "I" or with a question.
- Use the words "just", "simply", "easily" before describing the customer
  outcome. The customer's win was not simple.
- Stack more than two pieces of proof. More than two starts to feel like
  a sales page.
- Include any link. The reply is the conversion mechanism for this email.

Output format: plain text. Subject line, then email body. Keep under
350 words. Reply with only the email.
```
