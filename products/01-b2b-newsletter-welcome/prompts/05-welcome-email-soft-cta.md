# Prompt 5 - Welcome Email (Soft CTA)

**When to send:** Day 10.

**Purpose:** Close the welcome sequence with a low-pressure conversion ask. By day 10 the reader has gotten four emails of pure value. They are ready to consider your product, but only if you don't shove it at them.

---

## The prompt

```
You are writing day 10, the final email of a 5-email welcome sequence
for a B2B SaaS newsletter. This is the only email in the sequence
that asks for the conversion. It needs to feel like an invitation,
not a pitch.

Voice: warm but not sentimental. Direct about what you're offering.
Confident enough to let the reader say no.

Variables:
- NEWSLETTER_NAME = [NEWSLETTER_NAME]
- PRODUCT_NAME = [PRODUCT_NAME]
- PRODUCT_ONE_LINE = [PRODUCT_ONE_LINE]
- ICP_PAIN = [ICP_PAIN]
- SOFT_CTA = [SOFT_CTA]
- ICP_ROLE = [ICP_ROLE]
- PROOF_POINT_1 = [PROOF_POINT_1]
- TABOO_PHRASES = [TABOO_PHRASES]

What this email MUST do:
1. Acknowledge that this is the last email of the welcome sequence.
   One sentence. Don't make it dramatic.
2. Briefly recap what the reader has gotten over the last 10 days
   (3 bullet points max, very short).
3. Make the conversion ask. The ask is the product (or trial, or
   demo, or free tool - whatever SOFT_CTA points to). State what
   PRODUCT_NAME does in PRODUCT_ONE_LINE terms.
4. Include one piece of social proof - PROOF_POINT_1 in one sentence.
   Not a wall of testimonials.
5. Make the "no" as easy as the "yes". End with language like: "If now
   isn't the right time, no reply needed. I'll keep sending the
   Tuesday newsletter either way." This is critical - it gives the
   reader permission to disengage, which paradoxically increases
   conversions.
6. Sign off with the founder's name.

What this email MUST NOT do:
- Use any word in TABOO_PHRASES.
- Use urgency ("limited time", "only this week", "act now"). Soft
  CTAs work because they're soft. Urgency kills them.
- Hide the CTA behind a button image or a long setup paragraph.
  Make it text. Make it findable in 5 seconds.
- Open with "I wanted to reach out" or any other sales-intro phrase.
- Be longer than 350 words. The ask should feel lightweight.

Output format: plain text. Subject line, then email body. Under 350
words. Reply with only the email.
```
