# Prompt 1 - Welcome Email (Delivery)

**When to send:** Immediately on signup (within 60 seconds).

**Purpose:** Confirm the subscription, set expectations on cadence and content, deliver one piece of immediate value. No selling, no story, no fluff.

---

## The prompt

```
You are writing the first email in a 5-email welcome sequence for a B2B SaaS
newsletter. The reader just subscribed. They do not yet trust you. They are
deciding in real-time whether to keep you in their inbox or archive you.

Voice: direct, calm, specific. NOT friendly-for-the-sake-of-it. NOT salesy.
NOT hype-y. Imagine a senior practitioner writing to a peer.

Variables (substitute these):
- NEWSLETTER_NAME = [NEWSLETTER_NAME]
- PRODUCT_NAME = [PRODUCT_NAME]
- PRODUCT_ONE_LINE = [PRODUCT_ONE_LINE]
- ICP_ROLE = [ICP_ROLE]
- ICP_PAIN = [ICP_PAIN]
- NEWSLETTER_CADENCE = [NEWSLETTER_CADENCE] (e.g. "every Tuesday at 8am ET")
- SOFT_CTA = [SOFT_CTA]

What this email MUST do:
1. Confirm they're subscribed (1 sentence, no exclamation points).
2. Tell them exactly when the next email arrives.
3. Give them ONE specific piece of value they can use right now - a tip, a
   framework, a number, a link to your best back-issue. This is the
   "delivery" - they should get something real in exchange for their
   attention, not just a promise of future emails.
4. Tell them what to expect from this welcome sequence (the next 4 emails
   over the next 10 days).
5. One line of soft-CTA at the end. NOT a hard sell. SOFT_CTA goes here,
   verbatim or near-verbatim.

What this email MUST NOT do:
- Open with "Welcome!" or "Thanks for subscribing!" or any exclamation.
- Use the words "excited", "thrilled", "delighted", "glad", "honored".
- Include any emoji.
- Have more than 3 short paragraphs. New subscribers scan, they don't read.
- Mention pricing, features, or product capabilities beyond PRODUCT_ONE_LINE.
- Use em-dashes (-) anywhere except possibly in the subject line.

Output format: plain text. Subject line, then the email body. Keep total
length under 250 words. Reply with only the email - no preamble.
```
