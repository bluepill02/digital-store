# The B2B Newsletter Welcome Sequence

A 5-email welcome sequence for B2B SaaS newsletters, with the prompts that write it.

Built for B2B newsletter operators with **200-10,000 subscribers** who are tired of generic "Thanks for subscribing!" auto-responders that leak 40-60% of new readers in the first 7 days.

## What's in the bundle

- **5 ready-to-use prompt templates** (in `prompts/`) with [VARIABLE] slots for your specific product, audience, and proof points
- **The 5 emails they generate** (in `01-welcome-email-*.md` through `05-welcome-email-*.md`) as filled-in reference copies
- **2 fully worked examples** (in `examples/`) - one B2B devtools newsletter, one B2B fintech - so you can see exactly what good output looks like
- **`personalization-variables.md`** - the 12 inputs you fill once, then reuse across all 5 emails
- **`anti-ai-tone-checklist.md`** - 10 rules that keep the output from sounding like ChatGPT wrote it
- **`LICENSE.md`** - commercial use, single company, no reselling of raw prompts

## Who this is for

- Solo founders and small marketing teams running a B2B newsletter
- ICP is a specific professional role (DevOps lead, finance controller, RevOps manager, etc.) - not "everyone"
- You have at least 3 months of back-issues so new readers have something to read
- Your newsletter is tied to a SaaS product, service, or paid community - not pure media

## Who this is NOT for

- Consumer newsletters (different voice, different cadence)
- Newsletters with no product attached (no soft-CTA email applies)
- Anyone looking for a generic "10 ChatGPT prompts" pack - this is a workflow, not a list

## How to use it

1. Open `personalization-variables.md` and fill in the 12 variables for your newsletter. Takes 15-30 minutes.
2. For each prompt in `prompts/`, paste it into your LLM of choice with the variables substituted.
3. Run the output through `anti-ai-tone-checklist.md` before scheduling.
4. Schedule email 1 to send immediately on signup, then space the others across days 2, 4, 7, and 10.

## What this sequence is designed to do

- Reduce the % of new readers who ghost after the welcome email (the standard 40-60% leak)
- Establish credibility in the first 10 days before the reader's attention decays
- Set up a soft conversion path to your product without being salesy

## What this sequence is NOT designed to do

- Replace your core newsletter content (this only covers days 0-10)
- Generate hard-sell conversion emails (the CTA email is intentionally soft)
- Work for B2C newsletters (the voice is wrong for that)

## Format

You get a .zip with all files in markdown. Open in any text editor, paste prompts into your LLM, copy outputs into your email tool (ConvertKit, Beehiiv, Substack, Loops, etc.).
